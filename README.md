# Brazilian E-Commerce Analysis

## Project Overview
In this project, I worked on the **Olist Brazilian E-Commerce dataset** using **SQL, Excel, and Power BI** to understand how the business was performing across revenue, order trends, delivery timelines, product categories, and customer locations.

The main objective was to turn raw e-commerce data into a simple business dashboard that answers practical questions such as:
- How much revenue did the business generate?
- How did order volume change over time?
- Which categories were most frequently ordered?
- Where were most customers located?
- How did actual delivery compare with estimated delivery timelines?

## Tools Used
- **SQL** – for querying, aggregating, and analyzing e-commerce data
- **Excel** – for data cleaning and intermediate data handling
- **Power BI** – for building the final dashboard and presenting business insights

## Dashboard KPIs and Visuals
The Power BI dashboard focuses on a few core business metrics and trends:

- **Total Revenue** – A KPI card showing the total revenue generated from the order data
- **Delivery Analysis** – A comparison of estimated delivery days vs actual delivery days to understand delivery performance
- **Monthly Order Trend** – A line chart showing how total orders changed month by month
- **Top 10 Categories Ordered** – A category-level view of the most frequently ordered product categories
- **Customers by State** – A state-wise breakdown showing where the highest number of customers came from

## Key Insights from the Dashboard
After building the dashboard, these were the main observations:

- The dataset shows total revenue of **15.84M**
- Order volume generally increased over time, with visible growth in the later months
- A few product categories contributed a large share of total orders, with **bed_bath_table** appearing among the top categories
- **SP** had the highest customer concentration compared to the other states shown in the dashboard
- The dashboard indicates that actual delivery time was lower than estimated delivery time, which suggests delivery performance was better than expected in the summarized view
